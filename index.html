About Me

A scientific profile built around food science, natural antioxidants, analytical chemistry, and interdisciplinary research applications.

I am a final-year PhD researcher in Food Technology at Université d’Artois, France. My doctoral work focuses on the valorisation of mangrove resources and their impact on food quality, particularly through the extraction and characterisation of bioactive compounds such as polyphenols. I investigate how these compounds interact with food matrices and improve oxidative stability and functional performance.

My work integrates chromatographic, spectroscopic, microbiological, and statistical approaches, with applications in oils, seafood, and cereal-based products. I am interested in postdoctoral, research, and collaborative opportunities where I can contribute my expertise while continuing to grow through interdisciplinary science.
